Dear students of Berkely

You can do better. Your campus has been divided by ideological lines since the election of president Trump, a polarization that culminated a few months ago when the speech of conservative speaker Milo Yiannopoulos was canceled due to violent protests. Yet your predecessors fought for free speech on your campus fifty years ago, values that you now reject. Now with the coming of Ann Coulter’s speech on your campus, you have a chance to decide how you really feel about free speech. Will you allow her to express ideas you do not agree with or will you cancel her speech the same way you canceled Yiannopoulos’s speech? The choice is yours.

America's Polarization Problem

It’s no secret that free speech is under attack in America. For example, the speech of conservative speaker Ann Coulter was canceled by the Berkeley University of California since they couldn’t find a venue that provided sufficient protection for her. A similar so called “heckler’s veto” also shut down the speech of Milo Yiannopoulos a few months earlier. These two occurrences are part of a larger trend in America where conservative speakers cannot speak at liberal universities. While this trend is often portrayed as a free speech problem, the root of this problem is the way we do politics in this country. Politics has become a zero-sum game where one side wins at the expense of the other, a paradigm that we’re witnessing across colleges around the country.
